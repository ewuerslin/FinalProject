Troubleshooting Tips

Houseplants can be tricky to care for as a beginner since there are many variables that can affect the plant's health. Below are a couple tips to help you identify and troubleshoot common houseplant problems.

Leaves yellowing?
There a couple potential reasons this could be happening. Too much water, not enough water, or even just the natural aging of the older leaves.

Tiny gnats flying around?
Try adding cinnamon to the top of the soil and watering less and from the bottom.
Capture the gnats by leaving out a small dish of water with dish soap and apple cider vinegar mixed in!

No new leaves growing?
This could be caused by a lack a light. While in the winter this is typical, for the rest of the year, try moving the plant to a place with more sunlight.

Soil always dry?
Your plant might be ready to be repotted! Take a peak at the bottom of your pot, if you can see the roots coming out of the drainage hole, time to repot!
